# FenceTrace Contractor Outreach Kit

Target: owner-operators and small fence companies (1–3 crews). Audience skews
older and not tech-first — every word below is chosen to sound like a person,
not software marketing. Lead with money, never features.

The one-line pitch: **"You set your prices once; every estimate prices itself
and shows your profit before you send it."**

---

## Objection handling

- **"I do my estimates in my head / on paper."** — "And you're probably right
  most of the time. This just writes it down, counts the materials, and shows
  the customer something professional they can say yes to on the spot. Your
  numbers, not ours."
- **"I'm not good with computers."** — "That's most of my customers. If you
  can use Google Maps, you can use this. And I do the setup for you on a
  call — you watch, I type."
- **"What's it cost?"** — "Less per month than one shared Angi lead. No
  contract — if it doesn't pay for itself, cancel from your account page."
- **"I get all my work from referrals."** — "Even better — this isn't about
  finding strangers. It's about quoting your referrals faster than the other
  two companies they called, and knowing your margin on every one."
- **"Satellite won't be accurate on my lots."** — "It's for the quote, not
  the build. Get the job at a fair number in minutes, fine-tune on the site
  walk. You adjust everything before sending."

## Where to find them

- Facebook groups: fence contractor / fencing business owner groups (search
  "fence contractors", "fencing business"), local trades groups.
- Google Maps: search "fence company {metro}" — call the ones with websites
  that have no quote tool (that's nearly all of them).
- Supply houses: counter staff know every installer in town; ask about
  leaving cards.
- The compare page is the link to send: fencetrace.com/compare-fence-lead-costs.html

## Cadence

Email #1 → wait 3–4 days → follow-up → done (never more than 2 emails).
Track in a spreadsheet: company, contact, date, response. 20 contacts/week
is enough to learn what's landing; iterate the subject lines first.
