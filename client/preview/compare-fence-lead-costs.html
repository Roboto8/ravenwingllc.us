If you buy leads from Angi, HomeAdvisor, or Thumbtack, here is the math most contractors only do after the money is gone — and what the alternative looks like.

The sticker price isn't the real price

A fence lead from the big platforms typically runs $35–$100+. That sounds survivable next to a $4,000–$12,000 fence job. But the sticker price isn't what you actually pay, because:

1. The same lead is sold to 3–8 contractors. You're not buying a customer — you're buying a spot in a bidding war that starts the moment everyone's phone buzzes.

2. Around half never answer. Many homeowners fill out the form expecting an instant price, get a wall of phone calls instead, and go quiet.

3. You pay either way. Win or lose, ghost or no-show — the charge stands.
